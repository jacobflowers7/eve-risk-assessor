How Scoring Works

Every score is a percentile against the other tracked systems, so 80 means "riskier than 80% of Providence + Catch", not an absolute threat level. Pod kills are excluded (a gank would otherwise count twice) and NPC rats never count as attackers. Small samples are discounted: a system with 3 kills can't score like one with 300, no matter how bad those 3 look.

Activity
Kill rate, log-scaled — 10 kills/day saturates at 100.
Hunters
Share of kills by solo or 2–3 pilot gangs. The metric that matters most if you mine or rat.
Prey
Share of victims that were mining or hauling ships — proof that people like you get caught here.
Camping
How concentrated kills are in a few corps. High = resident campers, not passing traffic.
Gangs
Share of kills by 10+ pilot fleets.
Drops
Share of kills involving capital or black-ops attackers.

Overall = 25% activity, 20% hunters, 20% prey, 15% camping, 10% gangs, 10% drops. Data: recent killmails from zKillboard + ESI, cached locally and accumulated over time.
